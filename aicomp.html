Human Mind vs. Artificial Intelligence

While neural networks were designed to mimic the human mind, there are, of course, things that separate the two.

Differences

Size

Perhaps the biggest difference is the size. While neural networks can usually contain near 1000 nodes, the human brain has almost 86 BILLION neurons, allowing for far more considerations in how they process data at the cost of processing speed.

Differences

Learning

Thanks to the amount of neurons, the human mind is able to consider different interpretations of the same piece of data. By contrast, a neural network does not allow for such ambiguity.

Differences

Topology

While they may function in similar ways, the nodes of a neural network can only operate one by one, meaning that only node can be active at a time. By contrast, neurons can fire asynchronously, meaning that multiple nodes can be active at once.
