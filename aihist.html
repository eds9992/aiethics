What Is Artificial Intelligence?

Before we get into the ethics of artificial intelligence, we need do discuss what exactly it is an how it operates. In addition, we will also discuss the origins and evolution of artificial intelligence.

Beginnings

Conception

The concept of artificial beings have been around for centuries, dating as far back as the Greek Empire, with the giant Talos. Artificial intelligence as we understand it today has its roots in Alan Turing's "Theory of Computation," which states that by switching between 0's and 1's, a machine can simulate mathematical deduction. This theory would eventually lead researchers to theorize about building an electronic brain.

Beginnings

Development

The development that is considered to be the first artificial intelligence was developed by two researchers, Warren McCullouch and Walter Pitts, who made a formal design for Turing-complete artificial neurons. The field of AI research itself began in 1956 at the Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence, where attendees developed programs that learned strategies for checkers, solved word and algebra problems, and even learned English.

Beginnings

Evolution

As computers advanced, so too did AI. Faster computers and improvements allowed for advances in machine learning, with many companies adopting some form to collect and sort through increasing amounts of data. The year 2015 saw Google use AI on more than 2,700 projects; Bloomberg's John Clark attributes this increase to more accessible neural networks and a rise in cloud computing, research tools, and data sets.

How An AI Operates

Algorithms

Most modern day AIs use advanced algorithms to process and learn from data they take in. These algorithms help to train an AI on a specific dataset so that overtime, it will get better at identifying patterns in other similar datasets. The three most common algorithms used are neural networks, machine learning, and deep learning.

How An AI Operates

Neural Networks

Designed to mimic how our minds operate, one of the more common algorithms makes use of something called a neural network. These use a series of nodes that pass data to other nodes upon certain conditions. Initially, a neural network starts with two layers, the input and the output layers. Between these two initial layers, lie hidden layers where the given data is processed and passed between layers until it finally reaches the output layer.

Human Brain vs. Neural Network

As neural networks were designed after our own brains, there are similarities between their structures. Both have an output, an input, and various "layers" between them that process data to come to a conclusion. However, there are many differences between the two, which will be covered in the next section.
