The Measure of a Man

What makes a person? To help answer this, we will use the Star Trek episode, "The Measure of a Man." This episode centers around Commander Data's status, as either a person or an android. The trial has three central participants: Data(the defendant), Picard(defending Data), and Maddox(the plaintiff). Maddox argues that Data is not sentient and is therefore not entitled to the rights of living things. Picard asks Maddox what is required for something to be sentient, to which Maddox lays out three conditions he considers necessary for sentience.

Sentience

Intelligence

The first condition is intelligence. Intelligence is defined as the ability to acquire and apply knowledge and skills, something humans and animals can do. As AIs advance and learn, they may begin to learn new methods to more efficiently process data. In doing so, they would then fullfil the first condition.

Sentience

Self-Awareness

The second condition is self-awareness, which can be defined as knowledge as one's own existence. This knowledge can include knowing about our feelings and desires, and even a particular situation we may be in. Currently, known AIs lack self-awareness, as they are unable to think without data.

Sentience

Consciousness

The third and final condition is consciousness. While it may seem the same as self-awareness, there is a difference. Where self-awareness is the perception of a situation, consciousness can be seen as consideration of the situation, the "what" vs. the "why." Consciousness can be seen as asking why something is occurring, instead of simply observing it. To give an example, when we see something, self-awareness is the recognition that we see something. Consciousness, on the other hand, is us asking why we see that thing. "What allows us to see it?" "What happens in our eyes to see this thing?"
